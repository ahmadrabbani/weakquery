On June 20th, 2002 the British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC) claimed that human smuggling exceeded the drug trade as the world's largest illegal business.1 What are the implications of this change for the world and, in particular, for Europe?

I argue here that transnational crime organizations, which are becoming more competent in their trade, are responsible for the explosion of irregular migration in Europe. These transnational criminal organizations only recently became the subject of intense scrutiny and have therefore been able to firmly establish themselves in the countries in which they operate.
