Chapter 58: Personal And Real Property

Part 6.--MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

Article 25: Landlords And Tenants

Statute 58-25,124: Tenant's refusal to allow lawful access, remedies; landlord's unlawful or unreasonable entry, remedies. (a) If the tenant refuses to allow lawful access to the mobile home space, the landlord may obtain injunctive relief to compel access or may terminate the rental agreement. In either case, the landlord may recover actual damages.

      (b)   If the landlord makes an unlawful entry or a lawful entry to the mobile home space in an unreasonable manner or makes repeated demands for entry otherwise lawful but which have the effect of unreasonably harassing the tenant, the tenant may obtain injunctive relief to prevent the recurrence of the conduct or terminate the rental agreement. In either case, the tenant may recover actual damages.

      History:   L. 1992, ch. 306, § 26; July 1.
