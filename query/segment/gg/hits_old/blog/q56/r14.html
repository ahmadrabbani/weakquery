Before she was famous: Desperate Housewives' Teri Hatcher got her showbiz start by rocking the The Love Boat

By Laura Schreffler

Last updated at 7:41 AM on 26th September 2011

  • Comments (13)
  • Add to My Stories
  • Share

Teri Hatcher's career has gone swimmingly, especially given where she got her start.

The actress, now 46, appeared as Love Boat Mermaid Amy on the cult nineteen-eighties television show, The Love Boat.

The sitcom aired from 1977-1986 on ABC and followed the romantic adventures and mishaps of passengers and crew members aboard the Pacific Princess ocean liner.

A lovely beginning: Teri Hatcher got her showbiz start onthe cult nineteen-eighties television show, The Love Boat

Hatcher, who's most famous for her role as Susan Mayer on ABC's Desperate Housewives, appeared on The Love Boat from 1985-1986.

Life on Wisteria Lane has tamed Hatcher, who appears in more sophisticated ensembles on her current show.

Chances are she wouldn't be caught dead nowadays wearing Amy's spangled silver skimpy bodysuit costume.

Hello, sailor: The Desperate Housewives star used to wear spangled silver bodysuits

Rocking the love boat: Hatcher starred as mermaid Amy

Susan has become the brunette actress' signature role; she has won a Golden Globe and a Screen Actor's Guild Award for her portrayal.

Tonight marks the beginning of the end for Teri and her Desperate cohorts as its eighth and final season premieres.

The show also co-stars Eva Longoria, Felicity Huffman, Vanessa L. Williams, Marcia Cross and Brenda Strong.

Winning! The now 46-year-old actress won a Golden Globe for Housewives in 2005

All the Desperate women - aside from Hatcher, that is - were present and accounted for at the series finale premiere party in Hollywood last Wednesday.

There were rumours as to why she hadn't attended the bash, but no official word has been given regarding her absence for the evening.

The Wisteria Lane ladies still managed to have a good time and enjoy themselves at the event, held at Universal Studios, despite expectations that the show would continue until 2013.
