Greek Opposition Faces Internal Dissent Over EU Pledge

   By Costas Paris and Alkman Granitsas 
   Of DOW JONES NEWSWIRES

ATHENS (Dow Jones)--Greece's opposition New Democracy party is facing internal dissent over a demand by the European Union that it co-sign a letter committing it to implementing a new EUR130 billion bailout for the country, a senior party official said Tuesday.

The opposition, which is currently in talks with Greece's Socialist government on forming an interim government, has consistently opposed the austerity measures the Socialists have taken to win new fresh aid from Europe.

Earlier Tuesday, New Democracy leader Antonis Samaras gave his verbal support for the latest aid package but hinted he wouldn't sign such a letter--possibly throwing a fresh wrench in talks on a coalition government.

"Samaras is facing divisions within the party because a lot of senior party members feel that he is giving too much ground," the official said. "So a number of people are refusing to be part of the new government."

On Monday, euro-zone finance ministers met in Brussels to discuss Greece's aid package and decide on whether to disburse their portion of an EUR8 billion aid tranche promised to the country under a previous bailout.

Speaking to reporters after the meeting, Jean-Claude Juncker, head of the euro-zone finance ministers group, made clear he expected Greek political leaders to make explicit--and in writing--their commitment to implementing the details of the October aid plan.

At a Greek cabinet meeting earlier Tuesday, Greek Finance Minister Evangelos Venizelos said that such a written commitment would have to include signatures from both the government and the opposition, if it was to be accepted by the euro group.

According to officials present in the meeting, Venizelos said that a letter would have to include signatures from Samaras, Prime Minister George Papandreou, Greece's central bank governor, the finance minister and the new, interim prime minister. 


  -By Costas Paris and Alkman Granitsas, Dow Jones Newswires; +30 210 331 2881; alkman.granitsas@dowjones.com
