Iran's President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad says Tehran seeks full cooperation with Baghdad, reiterating that a stable Iraq would benefit Iran and the region.  “Iran seeks all-out cooperation with Iraq in line with the interests of the two countries as well as regional peace and brotherhood,” President Ahmadinejad said in a meeting with Nechirvan Barzani, the deputy head of the Kurdistan Democratic Party (KDP), IRIB website reported Thursday.  He said that Iran supports a well developed and stable Iraq, adding that stability and progress of Iraq would be to the benefit of Iran and the region.  President Ahmadinejad invited regional countries to unity and vigilance and said enemies cannot plot against regional countries as long as they maintain their unity.  He said that enemies of Iran and Iraq have been plundering the assets of the region over the past 100 years, adding that they are now plotting to create insecurity in the region.  The Iraqi official, for his part, praised Iran's efforts to help establish security in Iraq.  He highlighted relations with Iran and said Iraq is against any measure that would threaten the security of Iran.
