I don't know why you would, without a deeper look at the contextual issues. Could they have apprehended him, or a significant portion of AQ's senior cadre? Though the Taliban was an administration of sorts, who is to say they had any more control over Afghanistan's hinterlands than the NATO forces operating there with the new Afghan authority now?

Secondly, AQ attacking the United States still =/= the Taliban starting a conflict with the US. You can say that the United States had a pretext for initiating the conflict, but in doing so keep the language unambiguous. An existing pretext or causus belli isn't the same thing as the other party having 'started the conflict'. If we're talking about the physical conflict (and I believe we are) in Afghanistan now, it began when US forces initiated an offensive effort against the Taliban regime in 2001. I don't see how that's disputable, unless we conflate AQ and the Taliban. Which, to me, seems fairly disingenuous.

Like I said: I don't disagree with the NATO effort in Afghanistan. I just disagree with a fantasy approach to its context.
